Strategy
Brand Idea
The platform for autonomous mass — transforming individual drones into coordinated swarms across air, land, and sea.
Mission
Build swarms, not drones.
We transform individual drones into AI-enabled nodes of a larger network — creating resilient, coordinated swarms across air, land, and sea. Our open architecture gives democracies scalable, interoperable autonomy that resists jamming and wins at mass scale.
Vision
The leading platform for autonomous systems.
We envision millions of unmanned vehicles connected by one common operating system — swarming as a decisive force across defense, industrial, and civil domains, uniting allies and manufacturers into a shared ecosystem for autonomy.
Positioning
Auterion occupies a unique position at the intersection of interoperable architecture, AI-driven autonomy, and defense-grade reliability. Unlike proprietary systems that lock customers into single-vendor drones, Auterion provides the operating system that knits fleets together.
Open Architecture
Fosters interoperability and allows customers to integrate multiple manufacturers and payloads — enabling one operator to control many drones.
Battle-Proven
Products like Skynode S are resilient against jamming and turn inexpensive FPV drones into networked weapons at unprecedented scale.
Swarms, Not Drones
Differentiates by making large-scale autonomy accessible while maintaining ethical, democratically aligned partnerships.

Auterion delivers the operating system for swarms. AuterionOS provides an open architecture that integrates fleets from multiple manufacturers into a single, interoperable fabric. Our battle-proven technology lets vehicles lock onto targets and complete missions even when GPS and video links are jammed. Color Usage
Icons inherit the text color of their parent element. They are monochromatic by design — multi-color icons are not permitted in the system.
Correct
Ic
Icon inherits  currentColor  from its parent text color. Changes automatically with theme.
Incorrect
Ic
Never hardcode colors or use multiple colors within a single icon. Semantic color should come from the container, not the icon itself.

Two-Layer Architecture
Colors are organized into two layers. The first layer,  primitives , defines the raw chromatic scales &mdash; 32 scales with 12 steps each (2 custom Auterion, 6 Radix grays, 24 Radix chromatic). The second layer,  semantic aliases , maps intent-based names to those primitives. Components always consume semantic tokens, never raw scale values.
Layer 1
Primitives

--color-primary-subtle &hellip; --color-primary-text

Art Direction
Photographic style, mood, lighting, and composition principles for Auterion imagery — technical precision meets human purpose.
Visual Mood
Auterion photography bridges the gap between cutting-edge technology and meaningful human impact. Every image should convey quiet confidence, operational clarity, and the scale of autonomous systems working in concert. Avoid overly dramatic or cinematic looks — the technology speaks for itself.
Swarm in formation over terrain — calm, controlled, precise

Lighting
Natural light is strongly preferred. Golden hour and overcast conditions produce the most authentic results. Avoid heavy studio setups, artificial color gels, or over-processed HDR. When shooting indoors (labs, control rooms), use available ambient light supplemented with soft fill only where necessary.
Golden Hour
Warm, directional light that adds depth to outdoor drone operations. Preferred for hero and marketing imagery.
Overcast / Diffused
Even, soft light ideal for product detail shots and documentation photography. Reduces harsh shadows on hardware.
Ambient Indoor
Screen glow and workspace lighting for control room and lab environments. Keep it authentic, not staged.
Composition Principles
Strong compositions create visual hierarchy and leave room for typography and brand elements. Every image should feel intentional and structured.
Rule of Thirds
Place primary subjects at intersection points. Drones and operators should rarely be dead center unless conveying direct confrontation or symmetry.
Negative Space for Text Overlay
When shooting for marketing use, ensure at least one third of the frame is clean sky, ground, or blurred background suitable for headline placement.
Depth and Layers
Use foreground elements (terrain, equipment, people) to create depth. Swarms in the mid-ground with landscape beyond conveys scale.
Leading Lines
Runways, roads, flight paths, and formation lines naturally guide the viewer's eye toward the subject.
Color Grading
Desaturated base with selective color emphasis. The environment should feel grounded and real while Auterion's brand blue and product accents naturally stand out. Let the product and brand colors pop against a muted backdrop.
Base Treatment
Slightly desaturated, with lifted blacks and a cool-neutral tone. Reduce vibrancy of greens and browns in landscapes by 15-20%. Preserve skin tones.
Selective Color
Keep blues and teals at full saturation. LED indicators, UI screens, and brand elements should retain their original vibrancy against the muted background.

Text on social images must be immediately legible at mobile viewport widths. Less is always more — let the image do the work.
Maximum Word Count
No more than 6-8 words on any social image. If you need more context, put it in the post copy.
Font Size
Headlines: minimum 48px on the 1200px canvas. Subheadings: minimum 24px. Inter Semibold for all text.
Contrast
White text over a dark gradient overlay, or dark text on light image areas. Never place text directly on complex imagery without a backing gradient or solid bar.
Alignment
Left-aligned for LinkedIn and X. Centered for Instagram square posts. Never right-aligned or justified.
Safe Margins
Keep all text at least 60px from any edge. Account for platform UI overlaps (like/share buttons, profile pics in previews).
No Hashtags on Image
Hashtags belong in the post copy only. Never render hashtags as text overlays on the image itself.
Brand Element Placement
The Auterion logo and brand elements should be present but never dominant. They serve as a signature, not a watermark.
Logo Badge
Small Auterion symbol (not wordmark) in the top-left corner. White on dark images, dark on light images. Size: approximately 5% of image width. 24px margin from edges.
URL / CTA Bar
For campaign images only. Thin strip at bottom with auterion.com or a short CTA. Height: 40-48px. Background: brand blue (#1475ff) or semi-transparent dark.
Co-Branding
When partner logos appear, Auterion logo goes first (left or top). Equal sizing. Minimum 32px spacing between logos. Separate with a thin vertical or horizontal rule if needed.
Image Filter & Treatment
Social media images follow the same color grading principles as all Auterion photography, with minor platform-specific adjustments.
Standard Treatment
Desaturated base with selective color preservation (blues and teals). Slightly lifted blacks for a modern, editorial feel. Apply consistently across all posts.
High-Engagement Posts
For product launches or milestones, slightly boost contrast and saturation (10-15%) to stand out in feeds. Keep the desaturated background principle but let the subject pop more.
Avoid
No Instagram-style filters, no heavy vignettes, no artificial bokeh, no AI-generated imagery presented as photography. Keep it authentic.
Dark Mode Awareness
Many users browse in dark mode. Avoid images with large pure-white areas that create visual glare. Test images against both light and dark app backgrounds.

Capitalization

Headlines, page titles, and marketing materials use title case. Capitalize all words except articles (a, an, the), conjunctions (and, but, or), and short prepositions (in, on, at, for, to) unless they start the title.
Do

Lockups
Logo lockups define the fixed spatial relationships between the symbol, wordmark, and optional tagline. Never alter the proportions or spacing within a lockup.
Horizontal Lockup
The default lockup arrangement. The symbol sits to the left of the wordmark with a fixed gap equal to 50% of the symbol width. Use this in most applications, especially wide formats like website headers, presentation title slides, and signage.

Thousands Separator
Use commas for numbers with 4+ digits:  1,240  not  1240 . For numbers under 10,000 in tight UI contexts (badge counts, table cells), the comma may be omitted if space is constrained.
Decimal Precision
Match precision to the sensor or context. Coordinates need 4-6 decimal places. Altitude needs 0-1. Speed needs 1. Always use a leading zero:  0.42  not  .42 .
Date & Time
Use ISO 8601 for data interchange and logs. Use human-readable formats for UI display. Always use 24-hour time &mdash; AM/PM is ambiguous in multinational operations.

Timezone Handling
Store and transmit all timestamps in UTC. Display in the operator's local timezone with an explicit indicator:  14:32 UTC  or  14:32 (local) . In multi-timezone operations, always show UTC alongside local time.
Units & Measures
Always place a space between the number and unit. Use SI units as the primary system. Lowercase for unit symbols (m, km, kg) except where convention dictates otherwise (FL for flight level).

Aerospace Conventions
Domain-specific formatting rules that differ from general-purpose software conventions.
Heading & Bearing
Always 3 digits, zero-padded. No space before the degree symbol. Use &ldquo;T&rdquo; suffix for true north, &ldquo;M&rdquo; for magnetic when disambiguation is needed.
045&deg;
247&deg;T
003&deg;M
Altitude Qualifiers
Always specify the reference: AGL (above ground level), MSL (above mean sea level), or FL (flight level). Never display altitude without a qualifier.
120 m AGL
1,240 m MSL
Vehicle Identifiers
Use monospace for vehicle IDs, serial numbers, and callsigns. Keep consistent casing as defined by the source system.
AUT-2847
SKY-003
Mission Time
Elapsed mission time uses  HH:MM:SS  format, always zero-padded. For sub-second precision, append milliseconds with a period separator.
01:23:45
00:02:34.127

Weight Decision Guide
Three weights are sufficient for every interface. Adding more creates visual noise that slows operator decision-making.

Aa
Medium (500)

Aa
Regular (400)
